The weak form of the continuous Maxwell eigenvalue problem is given by: Find $\lambda \in \mathbb{R}$ and $u \in \HocurlM{\M}$ such that
\begin{equation}
  (\extd u, \extd v)_\M = \lambda (u, v)_\M \qquad \forall v \in \HocurlM{\M}.  
\end{equation}
Applying Loop subdivision alters the domain of the eigenvalue problem slightly. Thus, we cannot directly compare the obtained spectrum with the analytical solution. However, siscretisations using the FEEC complex $\FEECspaceZero{k}{}$ are known to converge to the spectrum of the analytical solution on the altered domain without introducing spurious modes (cf. \cite{Arnold.FEEC}). For this reason, we compare the spectrum obtained using the hierarchical subdivision $k$-form spaces with the FEEC spaces $\FEECspaceZero{k}{\L}$.

Figures~\ref{fig:Maxwell_exact_DoFmesh} and \ref{fig:Maxwell_exact_eigenvalues} present a DoF mesh and the eigenvalue spectrum using the subdivision $k$-form spaces defined on the DoF mesh. We observe that the spectrum is faithfully captured. This suggests that the underlying sequence of approximation spaces is exact.

On the other hand, Figures~\ref{fig:Maxwell_inexact_DoFmesh} and \ref{fig:Maxwell_inexact_eigenvalues} present a DoF mesh and an eigenvalue spectrum corresponding to a non-exact complex of subdivision $k$-form spaces. In this case, the discrete spectrum exhibits spurious modes compared to the FEEC discretisation.

\subsection{Vector Laplace problem}

The $1$-form Laplace problem is given by
\begin{equation}
  (\extd \boldsymbol{\delta} + \boldsymbol{\delta} \extd) u = f
\end{equation}
on a domain $\M$. Since the operator $\boldsymbol{\delta}$ is not well-defined for a (subdivision) $1$-form, we introduce the auxiliary variable $\boldsymbol{\delta} \sigma = u$ and use the FEEC-typical mixed weak formulation
\begin{align}
  (\sigma, \tau)_\M - (u, \extd \tau)_\M &= 0 \qquad \forall \tau \in \FEECspace{0}{}, \\
  (\extd \sigma, v)_\M + (\extd u, \extd v) &= f \qquad \forall v \in \FEECspace{1}{}.
\end{align}
